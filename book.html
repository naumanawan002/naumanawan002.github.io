The Help

By Kathryn Stockett

Summary

The Help is a novel by Kathryn Stockett published in 2009. Set in the early 1960s in Jackson, Mississippi, the book follows the story of a young white woman named Eugenia "Skeeter" Phelan who sets out to write a book about the experiences of black maids working in white households. The novel explores themes of racism, discrimination, and the struggle for civil rights during the Civil Rights Movement in the United States.

Why I Enjoyed This Book

  • The book sheds light on important issues of racial inequality and injustice.
  • The characters are well-developed and relatable.
  • The book is both engaging and thought-provoking.
